Trading Strategies

Five documented strategies with entry rules, stop placement, and target logic. Click any strategy to expand the full playbook.

M2B
Strategy 1
Moving Average 2nd Entry Long
With-Trend Long · EMA
High (trend) Long 2:1+ R:R
Trend Context Required
  • Strong uptrend - series of higher highs and higher lows. Price predominantly above EMA
  • Avoid if trend is weak, choppy, or sideways
Pullback Pattern
  • Two-legged pullback down to the EMA
  • First leg bounces, second leg tests the EMA again — the double-test confirms the level is holding
Why It Works
  • Institutions buy pullbacks to the EMA in uptrends
  • The second touch flushes out weak longs and attracts new buyers, creating a higher-probability entry point
Entry Trigger
  • Buy stop 1 tick above the high of the bar that touches the EMA on the 2nd leg down
  • Do NOT enter on the first leg — wait for the second test of the EMA
  • The signal bar should ideally be a bull bar or show a lower wick rejection at the EMA
  • If the entry is not triggered within 2 bars, cancel the order — the setup is stale
Stop Loss
  • Stop goes 1 tick below the low of the 2nd leg pullback
  • Move stop to breakeven once price has moved 50% of the way to the first target
  • Never widen the stop — if the thesis is wrong, take the loss cleanly
Target Logic
  • First target: prior swing high — exit 50% of position here
  • Second target: measured move — project the height of the prior bull leg from the pullback low
  • Move stop to breakeven after first target is hit. Let the runner work
Pre-Entry Checklist
  • Uptrend confirmed — HH and HL structure visible
  • EMA is rising (not flat or declining)
  • Two-legged pullback structure clearly visible
  • Signal bar is a bull bar or shows lower wick rejection
  • Risk is within 1-2% of account (size check done)
  • No major news event in the next 30 minutes
Skip the Trade If...
  • Trend is weak, choppy, or in a tight trading range
  • EMA is flat or pointing downward
  • Pullback is only one leg (this becomes a 1st entry)
  • Signal bar is a large bear bar closing on its low
  • Already taken 4 trades today (daily max reached)
  • Already down 15 pts today (kill switch triggered)
M2S
Strategy 2
Moving Average 2nd Entry Short
With-Trend Short · EMA
High (trend) Short 2:1+ R:R
Trend Context Required
  • Strong downtrend - series of lower lows and lower highs. Price predominantly below EMA
  • Avoid if trend is weak, choppy, or sideways
Pullback Pattern
  • Two-legged rally up to the EMA
  • First leg bounces up, small pullback, second leg rallies back to EMA
Why It Works
  • Institutions sell rallies to the EMA in downtrends
  • The second touch traps weak shorts who covered and new longs who bought the rally, fueling the next leg down
Entry Trigger
  • Sell stop 1 tick below the low of the bar that touches the EMA on the 2nd leg up
  • Do NOT short on the first leg up — wait for the second test
  • The signal bar should ideally be a bear bar or show an upper wick rejection at the EMA
  • Cancel the order if not triggered within 2 bars — setup is stale
Stop Loss
  • Stop goes 1 tick above the high of the 2nd leg rally
  • Move stop to breakeven once price has moved 50% of the way to the first target
  • Never widen the stop
Target Logic
  • First target: prior swing low — exit 50% of position here
  • Second target: measured move — project the height of the prior bear leg from the rally high
  • Move stop to breakeven after first target. Trail the runner
Pre-Entry Checklist
  • Downtrend confirmed — LL and LH structure visible
  • EMA is declining (not flat or rising)
  • Two-legged rally structure clearly visible
  • Signal bar is a bear bar or shows upper wick rejection
  • Risk is within 1-2% of account
  • No major news event in the next 30 minutes
Skip the Trade If...
  • Trend is weak, choppy, or in a tight trading range
  • EMA is flat or pointing upward
  • Rally is only one leg
  • Signal bar is a large bull bar closing on its high
  • Already taken 4 trades today
  • Already down 15 pts today
IB
Strategy 3
Inside Bar Breakout
Breakout Pattern
Med-High Both Directions 2:1+ R:R
Pattern Structure
  • B1 = Mother bar sets range. B2 = Inside bar (fully contained). B3 = Further compression. B4 = Trigger closes outside B3 range
Why It Works
  • Each bar builds energy inside the mother bar range. The final compression (B3) is a coil
  • When B4 closes outside B3, trapped participants on the wrong side exit, fueling the breakout
Entry Trigger
  • Long: Enter on the close of B4 when it closes above B3 high
  • Short: Enter on the close of B4 when it closes below B3 low
  • B4 must close outside B3 range — a close inside B3 is not a trigger
Stop Loss
  • Wide stop: 1 tick beyond B1 opposite extreme
  • Tight stop: 1 tick beyond B3 opposite extreme — tighter R:R but higher chance of being stopped out early
  • Move to breakeven once T1 (1:1) is hit
Target Logic
  • T1 (1:1): Project B1 full range from entry point. Exit 50% here
  • T2 (2:1): Double B1 range from entry. Exit 25% here, trail remaining 25%
  • Exit all if price re-enters B3 range after breakout — setup has failed
Skip the Trade If...
  • B4 closes inside B3 range — no trigger confirmed
  • B4 is a large outside bar engulfing B3 entirely
  • Breakout direction is counter-trend in a weak market
  • Any bar pokes outside B1 range before B4 triggers
FP
Strategy 4
Failure Patterns
Reversal Trap
Context Dependent Both Directions Advanced
Why It Works
  • When many traders get caught on the wrong side of a setup, they must exit
  • This forced exit flow drives the counter-move with conviction
Entry Trigger
  • H2 Failure: H2 buy fires, price reverses below signal bar — short below signal bar low once reversal confirmed
  • L2 Failure: L2 short fires, strong buying absorbs all sellers — long above signal bar high once reversal confirmed
  • Bear Micro-Channel: Keep shorting with the channel on every small bounce until exhaustion signal appears
Stop Loss
  • H2 Failure: Stop above signal bar high
  • L2 Failure: Stop below signal bar low
  • Bear Micro-Channel: Stop above prior bar high
  • Move to breakeven once 50% to target reached
Target Logic
  • H2 Failure: Measured move down or prior swing low
  • L2 Failure: Measured move up or prior swing high
  • Bear Micro-Channel: Continue until exhaustion signal (large bull outside bar, gap reversal, or close far above channel top)
Skip the Trade If...
  • Thesis is not clear
  • Setup is ambiguous or borderline
  • Risk is undefined
IRB
Strategy 5
Inventory Retracement Bar
Trend Continuation (Hoffman)
High (trend) Both Directions 2:1+ R:R
Definition
  • Single candle where open & close are 45%+ off high (bear) or low (bull)
  • Signals institutional reload complete
  • Candle color does NOT matter — only the position of open/close relative to bar extremes
Why It Works
  • Institutions accumulate/distribute inventory in stages
  • The IRB marks the reload moment — they have filled their counter-trend inventory and price is ready to resume
Entry Trigger
  • Long: Buy stop 1 tick above the high of the IRB candle
  • Short: Sell stop 1 tick below the low of the IRB candle
  • Price should break the IRB level within the next 20 bars
  • Beyond 20 bars: Signal degrades significantly — cancel the order
Stop Loss
  • Long: 1 tick below IRB low
  • Short: 1 tick above IRB high
  • Max risk: less than 1% of capital per trade
  • Move to breakeven once 50% to target reached
Target Logic
  • Lock 50% at 50% to target
  • Lock 80% at 80% to target
  • Lock 90%+ at major S/R (Fib, Pivot)
  • Trail remaining position in strong trend
Pre-Entry Checklist
  • 20 EMA at 45 degree angle
  • Higher timeframe must trend in same direction
  • Open & Close clearly 45%+ off extreme
  • Candle range less than ATR × 2
  • Risk less than 1% of capital
Skip the Trade If...
  • Trend is weak, choppy, or sideways
  • EMA is flat or pointing against trade direction
  • Higher timeframe not aligned
  • IRB range greater than ATR × 2
  • 4 trades already taken
  • Already down 15 pts (kill switch)
